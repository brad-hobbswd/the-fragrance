CHAPTER THREE

The Murmurs of Indignation

The fragrance lingered in the air like a living presence, wrapping the room in its sweetness and settling into every corner of the loft.

The aroma seemed to rest upon the walls themselves, weaving through warm light and drifting around every chair, every garment, and every quiet breath.

For several moments no one spoke.

Then the voices began.

At first the criticism arrived quietly beneath lowered breaths and exchanged glances.

"What a waste."

"That perfume could have been sold for fifty thousand dollars and given to the poor."

Their reasoning sounded sensible enough.

Elena heard every word.

Each sentence found its mark, yet she did not pull away or lower her eyes.

This had never been about money. This had never been about public approval. This was love.

Yet worship had never fit comfortably inside calculations.

Love had never been measured properly by numbers.

Then Joshua spoke.

"Leave her alone."

"Why trouble her? She has done something beautiful for Me."

The room grew still.

Tears slowly gathered in Elena’s eyes.

She had given everything she possessed, and somehow the fear she expected never came.

The offering had been received.

Her heart had been understood.

Nothing else mattered.

Holiness seemed to rest over the room while the fragrance still lingered in the air, quietly reminding everyone present that love poured out completely leaves a mark no criticism can erase.
